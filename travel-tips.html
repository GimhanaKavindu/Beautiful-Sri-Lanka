Essential Guides for Travelers

Plan ahead to maximize your island adventure experience securely with these quick tips:


  • Best Time to Visit: The west and south coast beaches are best from December to April, while the east coast shines from May to September.
  • Local Transportation: Riding the scenic trains (especially Kandy to Ella) is cheap and beautiful. Tuk-tuks are perfect for short trips.
  • Cultural Etiquette: Always dress modestly when visiting sacred Buddhist or Hindu temples (cover shoulders and knees), and remove shoes before entering.
  • Health and Safety: Carry a basic first-aid kit and stay hydrated. Be cautious when swimming and always follow local guidelines.
  • Local Cuisine: Don't miss trying authentic Sri Lankan dishes like rice and curry, hoppers, and fresh tropical fruits.
  • Accommodation: Choose from a range of options, from budget-friendly guesthouses to luxury resorts, ensuring a comfortable stay.
  • Carrying Cash: It's always a good idea to carry some local currency (Sri Lankan Rupees) for small purchases and emergencies.
  • Picture-perfect Spots: Don't forget to capture memories at iconic locations like the Sigiriya Rock Fortress, Ella's tea plantations, and the pristine beaches of Mirissa.
  • Natural Wonders: Explore the breathtaking waterfalls like Ravana Falls and the unique geological formations at Adam's Peak.
  • Wildlife Encounters: Take a safari in Yala National Park to spot leopards, elephants, and a variety of bird species in their natural habitat.
